/**
 * Thoughts about physical page frame allocation
 */
 
I can see 2 general approaches to this problem:
1) Use a bitmap with 1 bit per physical frame
2) Use a linked list or stack, with one (20-bit or 32-bit) entry per frame 

Challenge is to make operations on the frame allocator atomic without
using synchronization or locks

See http://www.iglu.org.il/lxr/source/include/linux/mm.h:
Linux uses the second approach, and includes fields for paging to disk.
The structure per frame is quite large (there is a 'TODO' to make 32 bytes),
and they take care to put commonly used fields together in cache lines.
(IMO it is really ugly, most fields go unused depending on how pages are used)

Actually, Linux uses a variant on (2) where frames are grouped according to the 'buddy'
distribution (powers of 2, 2^0..2^10), and put in a separate list per group
